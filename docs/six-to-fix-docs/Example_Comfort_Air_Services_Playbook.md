| **CHAPTER 2**  **Customer Strategy**  *Acquiring, Retaining & Growing the Right Customer Relationships* |

## **Strategic Overview**

Customer strategy is about knowing who your best customers are, attracting more of them, and building systems to retain them over time. For Comfort Air Services, the highest-value customers are homeowners who purchase service agreements and commercial accounts requiring scheduled maintenance. The customer strategy focuses on segmenting the database, personalizing communication, and building lifetime value through proactive service relationships.

## **Why Customer Strategy Drives Business Value**

* Service agreement customers have 3–5x the lifetime value of one-time repair customers
* A documented customer acquisition system reduces revenue volatility
* Segmented communication increases response rates and reduces unsubscribes
* Customer retention data is a key asset in a business valuation

## **Execution Blueprint**

**Digital Customer Acquisition**

* Google Ads — local search campaigns targeting high-intent terms (e.g., 'AC repair near me')
* Facebook Lead Ads — seasonal promotions with lead capture forms
* SEO content — blog posts targeting local HVAC search queries

**Customer Retention & Lifecycle**

* Service agreement enrollment campaigns — email and SMS sequences
* Annual maintenance reminders — automated seasonal outreach
* Post-service NPS surveys — measure satisfaction and flag at-risk accounts
* Loyalty and referral incentive programs

**Customer Segmentation Model**

| **CHAPTER 5**  **Sales Strategy**  *Creating a Repeatable, Scalable Revenue Engine* |
| --- |

## **Strategic Overview**

Sales strategy transforms marketing activity into predictable revenue. For Comfort Air Services, the highest-leverage sales improvements come from: (1) systematizing the lead-to-close process, (2) equipping technicians with simple upsell and enrollment tools at the point of service, and (3) building a pipeline visibility system that surfaces opportunities before they are lost. A documented sales process is also a key value driver at exit, as it demonstrates that revenue does not depend on any individual.

## **Why Sales Strategy Drives Business Value**

* A documented, repeatable sales process increases close rates and reduces dependency on top performers
* Pipeline visibility enables proactive management and revenue forecasting
* Technician-enabled selling (point-of-service upsell) is the highest-margin revenue in HVAC
* CRM-tracked sales history is a powerful tool in a business sale or valuation

## **Execution Blueprint**

**Lead-to-Close Process**

* Inbound inquiry → 5-minute response SLA → qualification → booked appointment
* Appointment confirmation sequence (email + SMS) to reduce no-show rate
* Technician consultation framework — structured on-site needs assessment
* Same-day quote delivery for installation and major repair work
* Quote follow-up sequence — Day 1, Day 3, Day 7 (email + call)

**Technician-Enabled Selling**

* Service agreement enrollment script and leave-behind materials
* Indoor air quality (IAQ) upsell checklist — deployed during every tune-up
* Equipment age and efficiency assessment — documented in CRM for follow-up opportunities

**Digital Sales Enablement**

* HubSpot deal pipeline — tracks every quoted job from inquiry to close
* Automated quote follow-up sequences triggered by deal stage
* Online booking capability reducing friction in the purchase decision
* Google Ads and Facebook retargeting for website visitors who did not convert

## **The HubSpot-First Architecture**

A critical strategic recommendation for Comfort Air Services is the adoption of a HubSpot-centric data flow: HubSpot → QuickBooks (not the reverse). This architecture has significant operational and strategic advantages:

| **HubSpot → QuickBooks Integration: Why It Matters**   * Traditional model (QuickBooks-first): invoices live in QuickBooks, marketing team has no visibility into customer revenue or service history * Recommended model (HubSpot-first): all customer interactions, deals, and revenue flow through HubSpot — QuickBooks syncs from HubSpot for accounting * Result: marketing automation can trigger based on actual revenue data (e.g., 'customer has not had service in 18 months' or 'customer has $X lifetime spend') * HubSpot's automation workflows can trigger renewal campaigns, upsell sequences, and win-back programs based on live invoice data from QuickBooks * This architecture enables a 360-degree customer view that is the foundation of data-driven marketing |
