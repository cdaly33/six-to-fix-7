No Leads Lead Nowhere
Leads are Necessary
Think about how important leads are. Unless you have an unending
referral network, and a 100% closing ratio, it is probably safe to assume
that your business can always use more leads. Most B2B and B2C
companies have experienced the dreaded empty sales funnel.
A Process is Consistent, Predictable
The best thing about referrals is that they are often qualified. However,
most customers and close contacts do not have an endless supply of
qualified referrals. So, queueing-up other ways to generate leads is
smart and a safeguard against times when referrals dry up.
Let’s assume your business requires 20 new customers per month, and
your sales team closes one in ten opportunities – a 10% closing ratio.
Let's also assume 1 out of 5 (20%) leads turns into a qualified prospect.
Your company "should" generate 1,000 leads per month to get to 200
“qualified" prospects per month (10 per day) to ultimately close 10% of
those opportunities. Needless to say, most companies would benefit
from a more consistent and predictable lead generation strategy.
Learn more about effective lead generation.
Bottom line: Hope is not a strategy when it comes to lead
generation.

Online Lead Generation
Many Questions Abound
Many companies look to online lead generation because it is often more
effective and less costly than events, direct marketing, or advertising.
Statista reports that in 2016, $1.9 billion dollars was spent on digital
lead generation in the United States.
Lead Generation Requires Strategy
If your company attends an industry trade show with 5,000 attendees;
you at least know that attendees are in your industry. Unfortunately,
events are the most expensive and ineffective method of gaining new
customers. Online lead generation makes sense, but the Internet has
750 million active websites. How will your audience find you? SEO
(Search Engine Optimization)? Pay-per-click advertising? Social media?
Direct mail? Email?
Many questions abound: how many leads do you need? What is
success? How do you reach your audience? What is the message? The
offer? The Call-to-Action (CTA)? How do we measure? What is the follow-
up? Who will follow-up? Effective lead generation requires a plan.
Learn more about lead generation strategy.
Bottom line: Lead generation requires strategy and planning.

The Funnel
The Funnel
The Sales “funnel” is a timless concept used by countless
organizations, both small and large. For direct sales teams and inbound
sales teams, the funnel helps reps, management, and the company
forecast, plan, manage, and deliver results.
The Funnel Has Fundamentally Changed
The funnel is typically viewed as a linear process; leads go into the top,
and the most qualified leads become opportunities, and drop out of the
bottom as “closed” sales, i.e. new customers. Like everything else, the
funnel has evolved dramatically.
Today’s funnel is hardly as
linear as we think it is;
prospects are typically much
further “down” the funnel
before they even speak to a
sales rep. At any given time, a
prospect could drop in the
funnel and be ready to close
without progressing from the
top of the funnel to the
bottom. The newer fluid
funnel reflects that prospects
self-inform by doing their own homework; exemplifying that content
## Page 12
plays a big role in the qualification, education, and overall sales
process.
Marketing and Sales Alignment
The funnel is now shared by
Marketing and Sales, and the
alignment between the two has
never been more important.
Why? Because Marketing is
driving much of the activity that
fills the funnel, and Sales
picks-up the prospects and
drives them toward the close.
But the very nature of these two
functions has changed
dramatically because of the
Internet and the nature of
“inbound.”
Push Versus Pull
The “push” world of
broadcasting messages to a large audience has evolved to a “pull”
mentality where brands are pulling prospects and customers to them
through content and education, resulting in inbound marketing and
inbound sales. Would you rather be pushed or pulled? Think about it.
Learn more about the fluid funnel and Marketing and Sales Alignment.

Know Your Audience
First Rules Are Often Simple and Difficult
Whether it is an audience of one or a thousand, you must know your
audience. Perhaps, the most difficult thing to do in marketing is connect
with and build an audience – literally and figuratively.
Audience First, Prospects Second
Reaching an audience has become increasingly difficult because there
are so many ways in which we can communicate; and so many ways we
can be ignored. Brands need to think about the audience differently,
more holistically, and less like prospects we just want to "sell."
Proctor and Gamble knew the audience would pay attention to a
commercial if it was wrapped inside a good story – so they developed
soap operas. People subscribe to media because they are interested in
the content; doesn’t it make sense to think about building a subscribed
audience for your business and then convert them into customers?
Maybe educate, inform, and entertain before selling?
Learn more about thinking differently about your audience.
Bottom line: A subscribed audience wants to connect with
you.

Learning as Marketing
Tell Don't Sell
It has often been said that most people like to make purchases, but do
not like to be sold. Think about the last time you became educated
before you made a purchase that required thought and consideration –
education engenders trust, confidence, and satisfaction.
Audience Has More Choices Than Ever
In virtually every industry, countless competitors exist and the
environment has drastically changed since the dotcom boom in the late
1990s and early 2000s. Since then, knowledge acquisition has evolved
at an unprecedented clip in the digital landscape.
Audiences are increasingly fragmented with exposure to thousands of
messages daily through more platforms than ever before in history.
People want to self-inform, expect companies to educate them, and
exert significant control over the sales process. Educating versus
selling can position your company as the leader, authority, and credible
resource in ways your competitors cannot or will not. Educating your
audience builds brand awareness, brand relevance, and brand loyalty.
Learn more about building your brand by building your audience.
Bottom line: Educate your audience, and they will repay you
with their business.

Personas
The Challenge of Reaching the Audience
Despite the prevalence of technology and platforms with which to
communicate, reaching an audience has become quite challenging.
Identifying the audience and how to reach them requires research.
Personas Can Help You Reach Your Target
Standard methods of segmenting an audience – demographic,
psychographic, geographic, and behavior are proven and timeless. A
Persona is a representation of your ideal customer, and allows you to
focus on the individual with specifics like job responsibilities and career
aspirations, beliefs, attitudes, how they acquire information, where they
hang-out online, as well as standard segmentation data.
Personas help you reach your ideal customer because you do the
research on who they really are and how to connect with them.
Segmentation such as Demographics can be misleading. For example,
18-34 year-old females have very different online habits, education,
families, professions, and career aspirations. A Persona enables online
marketing that is more realistic, empathetic, and effective.
Learn more about knowing your ideal customers through Personas.
Bottom line: Walk in their shoes; get to know your Personas.

Hearing and Listening
Two Different Things
The average US citizen is exposed to 2,000 - 5,000 commercial
messages per day. We hear a lot, but how many do we actually listen
to? Assuming you can reach your intended audience, does your
message resonate and actually capture their attention?
Personas Help You Develop Your Message
Think about the marketing most companies conduct, and the messages
to which you are exposed every day. How much of it is self-serving info
about the company, brand, or product versus the audience? How much
of it is spouting promotions, features, and otherwise nondescript
messaging? How much is actually relevant or even interesting to you?
Personas help define messaging, because we become more intentional
about what we are saying. For example, time may be a more significant
challenge to a business owner than say, the cost of a solution, so why
message about price if that is the case? Relevant messaging is more
likely when you step into your Persona’s shoes.
Learn more about messaging ideal customers through Personas.
Bottom line: Think about what they want to hear versus what
you want to say.

Customer Journey
Help Them Along the Journey
Personas help marketers know and empathize with prospective
customers. Additionally, Personas help identify the journey  a prospect
takes in becoming a customer, and how to assist them on their journey.
The Path is Usually Not a Straight Line
For any kind of considered purchase, a purchase decision typically
involves more than simply hearing about a product or service. The
standard consumer decision-making process starts with recognizing a
problem or need; conducting research; evaluating alternatives; making
a purchase; and post-purchase behavior. Inbound Marketing simplifies
the steps into Awareness, Consideration, and Decision stages.
The “customer journey” (or "buyer's journey") gives prospects the right
information at the right time through the right medium during their
journey. For example, at the beginning of the journey, a purchase offer
doesn’t make sense; they need more information. Conversely, a
prospect ready to purchase needs information on why a solution is the
best choice, not information helping them determine their need.

Bottom line: Messaging is not one size fits all and needs to

Calls-to-Action
Then What?
As you strategize about building your business, and developing a
marketing strategy for prospects and customers, it is critical to
consider offers or calls-to-action (CTA) leading them along their
Customer Journey. Intentional marketing means being intentional about
next steps. The Customer Journey will be fraught with missteps and
wrong turns if we don't ask ourselves, "Then what?"
Action Requires Reaction
Let’s say a communication captivates you, but there is no clear call-to-
action. If you are confused on what to do next, that is a
compromised customer experience. That initial communication is the
beginning of the customer experience; not when you actually contact
the company or visit their website.
An impression is formed the instant we interact with a brand. As
business owners and executive managers, it is our responsibility to
provide compelling and relevant messaging, but also clearly directing
prospects and customers with clear CTAs for next steps on their
Customer Journey.
Learn more about CTAs in your digital marketing.
Bottom line: The Customer Experience includes CTAs.

Follow-Up
Failure to Follow-up
It is reported on average, that it takes eight attempts to reach a
prospect, yet 44% of salespeople give-up after one follow-up, and the
average sales person only makes two attempts to reach a prospect.*
The Key to Increasing Sales
Imagine a prospect contacts your company, and Sales doesn’t follow-
up, or does so in a tardy manner. Or, a qualified prospect is interested,
but just not ready to purchase now, so nobody follows-up. Two common
examples of follow-up failure that plague many Sales teams.
Estimates indicate that 50% of qualified leads are not yet ready to
purchase.** Prospects do not always purchase on our timeline; short-
term thinking - centered around this month’s sales goal, leads to a
broken funnel strategy, incomplete Customer Journey, and lost
opportunities.
