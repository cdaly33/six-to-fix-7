**6 To Fix Report Wish List—Situation Analysis**

1. **Situation Analysis**
   * **Internal** 
     1. **Mission, Vision, and Goals**: Clarify the organization’s overarching purpose, future aspirations, and specific objectives.
     2. **Product and Service Portfolio**: Review current product lines or service offerings, product lifecycles, and areas of differentiation.
     3. **Resources and Capabilities**: Assess human resources, financial standing, brand reputation, technological infrastructure, and operational efficiencies.
     4. **Past Performance and Current Results**: Examine recent sales trends, market share, profitability, and any key metrics (e.g., customer acquisition cost, customer lifetime value).
     5. **Key Internal Challenges**: Identify where the organization struggles internally, such as capacity constraints, talent gaps, or inefficiencies.
   * **External**
     1. **Market Size and Growth**: Understand the current size of the market, growth rate, and emerging trends.
     2. **Customer/Consumer Analysis**: Investigate consumer demographics, psychographics, behaviors, purchasing patterns, and evolving needs.
     3. **Competitive Landscape**: Identify direct and indirect competitors; assess their market positions, strengths, weaknesses, and strategies.
     4. **Distribution and Supply Chain Factors**: Evaluate relationships with distributors, suppliers, and retailers; note any supply chain constraints or competitive advantages.
     5. **Regulatory and Legal Considerations**: Recognize relevant laws, regulations, and compliance factors that affect operations or growth potential.
2. **Environmental/Contextual Factors**
   * **Industry Trends**: Note macro-level shifts, such as emerging technologies, cultural trends, environmental pressures, or policy changes.
   * **Socioeconomic Conditions**: Assess consumer confidence, spending patterns, and broader economic indicators (e.g., GDP growth, unemployment rates).
3. **SWOT Analysis**
   * **Strengths**: Internal capabilities and resources that give the organization an advantage.
   * **Weaknesses**: Internal limitations or areas of underperformance that may hinder success.
   * **Opportunities**: External trends or changes in the market/environment that the organization can leverage.
   * **Threats**: External challenges or barriers (e.g., new competitors, regulations, changing customer preferences).
4. **Key Insights and Problem Definition**
   * **Key Insights:** Positive, negative, or neutral considerations/observations, etc. that can affect the business, brand, products, customers, etc.
   * **Major Issues and Risks**: Summarize the most pressing challenges emerging from the internal/external analyses.
   * **Opportunities for Growth**: Highlight the strongest avenues for market expansion or new product development.
   * **Strategic Implications**: Translate the findings into strategic considerations for the organization—e.g., how to capitalize on strengths, mitigate weaknesses, and navigate environmental factors.
   * **Risks to Success**: What things potentially stand in the way of achieving success?

**Why These Elements Matter**

* **Strategic Alignment**: Ensuring the organization’s mission, objectives, and actions align with what’s happening in the market.
* **Risk Management**: Identifying potential threats and planning how to mitigate them proactively.
* **Competitive Advantage**: Recognizing strengths and market opportunities that can help the organization stand out.
* **Informed Decision-Making**: Backing up choices (e.g., resource allocation, product development) with data-driven insights.

By including these core areas in a situation analysis, organizations gain a clear, comprehensive picture of where they stand and how they can move forward effectively.
