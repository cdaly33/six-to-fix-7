## Audit Probes by Area

The following table presents the approved primary question, two tailored follow-up probes, and the intended focus for open-notes capture in each of the six framework areas.

| **Area** | **Primary Question (1–5 Scale)** | **Probe 1** | **Probe 2** | **Open Notes Intent** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Brand** | How clearly can you articulate *why* a customer should choose you over alternatives? | What specific difference matters most to your best customers—is it reliability, expertise, price, or something else they consistently mention? | If a customer were introducing you to a friend, what would they say is unique about your company? | Position, differentiation, perceived value vs. direct competition, brand promise clarity |
| **Customer** | How well-segmented and understood is your customer base, and how deliberately are you building lifetime value? | What's the profile of your highest-value customer—how do they differ from one-time or low-value customers in terms of purchase size, frequency, or type? | How intentionally are you staying in touch with customers after the initial sale, and what are you doing to encourage repeat business or referrals? | Customer segmentation, acquisition strategy, lifetime value mechanics, retention discipline, referral sources, churn patterns |
| **Offering** | How clearly do your offerings map to customer problems, and are they priced to reflect value (not just cost)? | Walk me through your three most important offerings—what customer problem does each solve, and what does a customer actually *get* (not just what you deliver)? | Do you have different ways customers can buy from you (e.g., à la carte vs. packages, subscription vs. one-time), or is there a single path to purchase? | Product/service mix, problem-solution mapping, pricing logic, bundling strategy, upsells, value clarity in customer terms |
| **Communications** | How consistent and strategically targeted is your customer communication across owned channels (website, email, social)? | What are your top three marketing channels right now—which ones bring in actual customers, and which ones are you doing because you feel you "should"? | If you stopped all marketing tomorrow, which communication or content has the most lasting power—what's your real owned asset? | Channel effectiveness, owned vs. paid balance, messaging consistency, content strategy, SEO/search visibility, first-party data capture |
| **Sales** | How systemized is your sales process, and how aligned are marketing and sales on lead handoff and qualification? | When a prospect becomes interested, what actually happens next—do they talk to someone, get a proposal, go through a structured process, or is it loose? | Do you know what percentage of prospects actually convert, and where deals typically stall or fall apart? | Pipeline clarity, sales discipline, marketing-sales alignment, conversion rates, bottlenecks, proposal/follow-up discipline |
| **Management** | How documented, measurable, and owner-independent is your marketing operation? | What marketing metrics do you review monthly—revenue, leads, customer acquisition cost, retention rate, or is it more activity-based (posts, calls, emails)? | If you were not here next month, could someone else step in and run the marketing system you've built, or does it all depend on you? | KPI documentation, measurement discipline, process repeatability, accountability, owner-independence, system durability |

## Design Philosophy

### What These Probes Do

1. **Probe sideways, not downward.** Each probe does not mechanically repeat the primary question. Instead, it surfaces specific business behavior, systems maturity, and strategic gaps the client may not see.

2. **Generate playbook-actionable intelligence.** Every probe was designed to produce answers a consultant can use *immediately* to build a tailored playbook chapter. For example:
   - Customer probe → customer personas → retention strategy chapter
   - Communications probe → content audit + channel prioritization
   - Sales probe → pipeline documentation + sales process design

3. **Stay conversational for a 90-minute call.** Two probes per area (12 total) + open notes fit comfortably in a discovery call without overwhelming the consultant or the client.

4. **Ground in source material frameworks.** Each probe references strategic concepts from the 6 To Fix source materials:
   - **Brand:** Aaker's distinction between "preference" and "relevance"; internal brand alignment
   - **Customer:** CLV/CAC mechanics; share-of-customer growth; repeat customer economics
   - **Offering:** Value-first language; outcome vs. feature framing; problem-solution fit
   - **Communications:** Owned audience vs. rented platforms; inbound vs. outbound; first-party data
   - **Sales:** Marketing-sales alignment; pipeline visibility; sales process discipline
   - **Management:** System documentation; KPI clarity; owner-independence (transferability)

## Probe Design Rationale by Area

### Brand
**Primary Question:** How clearly can you articulate *why* a customer should choose you over alternatives?

**Probe 1 Intent:** Surface the specific customer value claim (not generic positioning). What do actual customers say is different about you?

**Probe 2 Intent:** Test authenticity and customer voice. How would a loyal customer describe you—does it match your positioning, or reveal a gap?

---

### Customer
**Primary Question:** How well-segmented and understood is your customer base, and how deliberately are you building lifetime value?

**Probe 1 Intent:** Identify the highest-value customer archetype. This becomes the core persona for playbook segmentation and retention strategy.

**Probe 2 Intent:** Reveal retention mechanics and intention. Are post-sale activities deliberate (system) or accidental (activity)?

---

### Offering
**Primary Question:** How clearly do your offerings map to customer problems, and are they priced to reflect value (not just cost)?

**Probe 1 Intent:** Test outcome language and problem clarity. Can the owner articulate what customers *get* (outcome) vs. what you *do* (activity)?

**Probe 2 Intent:** Reveal pricing and bundling sophistication. Is there pricing strategy or just a price list?

---

### Communications
**Primary Question:** How consistent and strategically targeted is your customer communication across owned channels (website, email, social)?

**Probe 1 Intent:** Separate intention from activity. Which channels actually produce customers (owned asset building) vs. busy work?

**Probe 2 Intent:** Identify the true owned asset. What sticks when paid media stops? (This becomes the foundation of long-term communication strategy.)

---

### Sales
**Primary Question:** How systemized is your sales process, and how aligned are marketing and sales on lead handoff and qualification?

**Probe 1 Intent:** Document sales process maturity. Is there a system, or does each deal follow its own path?

**Probe 2 Intent:** Reveal pipeline visibility and bottlenecks. Conversion metrics are a diagnostic—where does the process break?

---

### Management
**Primary Question:** How documented, measurable, and owner-independent is your marketing operation?

**Probe 1 Intent:** Identify KPI maturity. Are metrics outcome-focused (revenue, CAC, retention) or activity-focused (posts, calls)?

**Probe 2 Intent:** Test transferability and documentation. This is a critical valuation factor and playbook anchor.

## Approval Checkpoint

**The primary questions are locked.** The framework is locked: six areas, one blended 1–5 score per area, two short free-text probes per area, plus open notes per area. Scores are not aggregated or weighted; each area stands alone as a 1–5 assessment.

**What requires Chris's approval before the audit form is fully locked:**

1. **Do the probes feel natural and practical for a 90-minute discovery call?** Wording should flow conversationally, not sound clinical or repetitive.

2. **Will these probes generate enough specificity to write a tailored playbook?** The consultant should walk away with concrete insights about the client's Brand position, Customer segments, Offering mix, Communications assets, Sales process, and Management discipline.

3. **Is the tone and assumption level right?** Should any probe be more directive, or less assumptive of existing systems? (E.g., "do you have a CRM?" vs. "what systems do you use for tracking customer interactions?")

Minor wording adjustments to probe language are welcome. Major strategy shifts (e.g., "add a third probe per area," "change what we're measuring," "separate Customer into Acquisition and Retention") should not happen at this stage—the framework is solid and ready to pilot with a real client.
