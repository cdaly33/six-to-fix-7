Goals and Expectations
What To Do
Marketing budgets are increasingly digital, but offline and integrated
strategies continue to be relevant to numerous customer segments
within a variety of industries.
Different Environment, Different Thinking
More sophisticated marketing departments have been measuring sales,
market share, share of customer, and countless ROI metrics for ages.
However, newer, less robust and digital marketing departments now
enjoy a leveled playing field due to ever evolving technology, analytics
tools, and…management demands. But what should be measured?
It’s still about one thing: the audience. Identifying, reaching, and
measuring the audience however, brings many challenges.
Understanding how current customers and prospects interact in the
digital world, and recognizing that most purchasing processes are
anything but simple or linear. Nurturing the audience from prospect to
customer to advocate is the “new” marketing. How should things be
measured? By whom and with what tools? What does success mean?

Marketing is Research
Starts with Questions
Research has largely been reserved for companies with large budgets
and a decent-size teams. But, research is readily available to virtually all
companies; just need to ascertain what you want to know, the tools to
use, and what actions you want to take based on the research.
Research is Marketing
Data and analytics are often thought of in the context of measurability
and results. But, the digital landscape has enabled continuous research
all the time...in real time.
Data is abundant…but what are the useful metrics to drive the business
forward?
For example, your website is a live research tool: unique visitors, page
views, forms submitted, links clicked, videos viewed, ebooks
downloaded, etc. Technology is the enabler; research starts with
questions and hypotheses. The real opportunity lies in using these tools
to inform, disprove, or validate questions we seek regarding prospects,
customers, site usability, customer experience, sales, ROI, and more.
Learn more about research for more effective marketing.

What Do You Need to Know?
Insight Breeds Foresight
Retail pioneer, John Wannamaker famously said, “I know half of my
money is wasted on advertising; I just don’t know which half.” Many
companies view marketing as an expense versus a strategic weapon,
and attribution (cause and effect) has historically been difficult to report
and in many cases, still is.
What to Measure, What Not to Measure
Indeed, that is the question. The first step? Determine what you need to
know and what you don't. What is working and what isn’t? It seems
counter-intuitive because we love success, but it is just as important if
not more so, to know why something worked as opposed to simply
focusing on what didn't work.
Google Analytics, HubSpot (Marketing Automation), CRMs, and tools like
DOMO empower businesses to understand their marketing efforts.
Analytics provide insights for current efforts and proactive, data-
driven marketing in the future. Data should provide insights; too much
data can actually hinder marketing, steal focus, and thwart progress.
Learn more about marketing effectiveness.

important as why something isn't.

Audience
The Audience First
Used to be and in many cases still is that results were all that mattered.
Well, Sales has changed a lot, and more effective marketing means
understanding how the game has changed...significantly.
How people purchase, communicate, and interact with media directly
relate to establishing relationships with them so you can "sell" them.
The process by which people become aware, self-inform, shop, and
purchase has completely transformed and will continue to do so.
How Well Do You Know Your Customers?
To improve marketing effectiveness, we need to focus on people first,
then the efficacy of specific tools, tactics, and campaigns.
What about the audience? Does our messaging still resonate? Is the
message reaching the intended audience? Do the mediums through
which we communicate still make sense? Are we making assumptions
or “going with our gut”? Audience knowledge improves the customer
experience, results, competitive advantage, and ROI.
Learn more about building your audience.

Analyses
In the Dark.
“We just need our marketing to do more.”
“We’re spending a lot of money on marketing, but we don’t really know
how effective it is.”
"We spend a lot on our website; not sure how much it helps us."
Common refrains on marketing. Naturally, such statements elicit a host

Marketing is Multi-Faceted. So is Data.
An obvious first-step analysis includes current strategy; how is it being
executed? Does the current strategy and execution support the
company goals and objectives? What are the current digital marketing
efforts? Website? SEO? Social? Are there technologies like Marketing
Automation and CRM systems employed in your current efforts?
What is being measured? How are current efforts measured, analyzed,
and evaluated? What does success really look like?

Marketing and Technology
Inbound Marketing
A lot of buzz has taken place over the last few years around “inbound
marketing,” especially from marketing automation platforms like
HubSpot, Marketo, Hatchbuck, and others. A fundamental shift has
occurred in the way many companies market; and the ways to reach
intended audiences continue to evolve.
Building an Audience to Grow Your Business
We hear a lot about “digital,” and how companies are spending more on
digital marketing. True. But, it’s less about digital, as it is reaching
audiences in new and various ways enabled by digital technology.
Marketing and technology are inseparable; digital tools and data
provide marketing that is smarter, faster, more efficient, and more
effective...or at least should be.
Note, we didn’t say, “easier.” Knowing "what" to do is a big challenge.
Marketing must be approached differently because behaviors have
drastically changed, and will continue to evolve in new and significant
ways because of technology.

## Page 19
The Website
Your Website and Business
Your website is a critical business asset that needs to be thought of
strategically; nurtured, grown, and respected. Your website is your
business even if it doesn't directly drive revenue. Do you know how your
website is impacting your business?
Build a Brand Platform: Your Website
Social media is obviously a thing. Search Engine Optimization (SEO) can
help with search rankings and traffic. Pay Per Click (PPC) can drive
traffic if the budget allows it. YouTube is the second largest search
engine on the web. Amazon is an Internet unto itself.
Guess what? Your company cannot own or influence how these
platforms are managed. Google makes changes all the time. Amazon’s
customers are their customers, not yours. What can you control? Your
website. It gives information. Gets information. Sells prospects and
customers. Gets shared with others. Provides results through various
tools. It’s awesome. Potential effectiveness often taken for granted.
Learn more about inbound marketing and technology.
