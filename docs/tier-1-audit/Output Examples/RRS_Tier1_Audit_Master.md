# **2. Methodology & Evidence Base**

### **The 6 To Fix Marketing Strategy Framework**

The audit scores Rolling Rock against the same 6 To Fix Marketing Strategy Framework that organizes the entire Strategic Glue engagement. Six strategic areas — Brand, Customer, Offering, Communications, Sales, Management — are assessed on two independent criteria:

* **Activity Score (0-10)** — quality and maturity of the marketing work happening in this area.
* **Documented Strategy (None / Partial / Current)** — whether the strategic thinking behind the work is written down, updated within 12 months, and used in actual marketing decisions.

These criteria are independent. A client can have high activity and zero documented strategy, or substantial documentation that no one references. The rubric surfaces the difference because the remedy is different. Activity gaps are closed by execution; documentation gaps are closed by capturing and codifying what already exists in operating practice.

### **Composite scores**

* **Total Marketing Maturity** (0-60) — sum of the six Activity Scores.
* **Documented Strategy Coverage** (0-6) — count of areas where Documented Strategy is rated Current.
* **Systems Maturity Score** (0-20) — independent four-dimension assessment of how the marketing operation runs as a system, regardless of activity quality.

### **Evidence base**

Every score in this audit is supported by at least two evidence citations from the project knowledge. The evidence base for Rolling Rock comprises:

* 12 sales team and stakeholder interviews conducted by Strategic Glue (Adam, Brian, Ethan, Fred VanNess, Gary Hallman, Isaac Davis, John Indy, Joe Zimring x3, Randy, Sean, Terrence, Winnie) — full transcripts and call summaries available in the engagement file.
* Comprehensive product collateral inventory: 10 product brochures (Building Stone, RSV, TSV, Brownstone, Dorchester, Midnite, Landscape, 2016 BS Pro), the Building Stone Style Guide, and the RRBS Booklet.
* Terry Sullivan's competitive analysis (May 2026) covering QuarryCut, Delgado Stone, and SiteOne — Joe's three named competitors — across all six 6 To Fix areas.
* Ben Sullivan's expanded comparables roster (Comparables V3, May 2026) covering ~150 net-new prospect firms across five segments within a 200-mile radius of Boyertown.
* Ben Sullivan's social and digital presence audit (April 2026) covering Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Google Business Profile, YouTube, Pinterest, Reddit, and competitor benchmarking.
* rollrock.com website inventory and product taxonomy review.

### **Calibration**

The methodology is calibrated against a reference set of prior audits. For owner-operated businesses with $5M-$10M in revenue, total Marketing Maturity typically lands between 18 and 32 out of 60, and Documented Strategy Coverage typically lands at 0 to 2 of 6. Rolling Rock's results — 22/60 marketing maturity and 0/6 documented strategy coverage — fall within these calibration bands. Scores were assessed without reference to the calibration set first, then checked for systematic drift.

### **What this audit is, and is not**

This audit is a structured diagnosis built from evidence in the project. It scores against a defined rubric, surfaces gaps with citations, and produces a prioritized roadmap. It is not a strategy document — that is the Tier 2 deliverable. It is not an execution plan — that is what Months 4-12 of the engagement deliver. It is the foundation against which both downstream deliverables are built.

# **5. Systems Maturity Score**

Systems Maturity is an independent cross-cutting score that asks how the marketing operation runs as a system, regardless of how mature the activity is in any individual area. The four dimensions are scored independently 0-5, then summed to produce a 0-20 total.

The distinction matters: a client can have strong activity in several areas while the underlying operation is not a system — because nothing is documented, nothing is repeatable without the owner's attention, and nothing transfers when the owner steps away. Systems Maturity captures that property directly.

### **Score: 6 / 20**

Rolling Rock's Systems Maturity sits below the 8/20 typical for first-time audit clients in this revenue band. The score reflects the structural reality of an owner-operated 71-year business that has built market position through Joe's direct involvement and the institutional knowledge of senior sales staff: the marketing function is fully owner-dependent, marketing measurement is largely absent, almost nothing runs as a repeatable process, and documentation exists in pockets (product catalogue, competitive analysis) rather than as a system. Building the systems layer is the central long-term objective of the engagement.

The Tier 3 fCMO+AI engagement is correctly scoped to move this score into the 8-12 partially systematized range over the next 12 months and toward 13+ over the longer term.
